Generate a pattern

In this task, you will learn how to generate a pattern for a problem by reading an example. After the example we will ask you to generate patterns for two problems, one by one.

Problems share patterns which describe the structure and essence of problems abstractly. In this task, we would like you to generate a pattern for a problem. We will first demonstrate the process of generating a pattern through an example.

An example problem: In cold weather, you often find single dropped gloves lying on the street lonely. Almost everyone, not only kids, has lost one of their gloves, leaving the other useless. It is frustrating and a waste of money. How can we solve the problem of losing gloves?

A similar problem: Women like wearing earrings. But they get lost very easily. There's nothing more frustrating than looking in the mirror and realizing you've lost an earring or going to grab a pair from your dresser and finding only one. How can we solve the problem of losing earrings?

By comparing the gloves-dropping problem with the earrings dropping problem, we can generate a pattern for the gloves-dropping problem like this: Some objects need to work together in a pair. The problem is one of the pair is often lost leaving their partner useless. How can we prevent losing one of the pair?
